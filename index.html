Research & Development Projects

Car Speed Checker and Overspeed Detector

This project addresses the critical issue of overspeeding on highways. We developed a system using an Arduino Uno microcontroller and two infrared sensors to detect vehicle speed and display it on an LCD. The system alerts drivers to overspeeding by activating a buzzer. It calculates speed based on the time taken to travel a fixed distance between two IR sensor points. The concept can be expanded to include security cameras for monitoring driver conduct and identifying license plates for traffic authorities.

Tools: Solderless Breadboard, Arduino Uno, IR Sensors, 16*2 LCD Display, 4.7K Resistor, Jumper Wires, 9V Battery & Clip, IR Sensor Module.

Outcome: A functional prototype demonstrating real-time speed detection and overspeed alert capabilities.

View Report →

1250kVA Transformer Design

As part of the electrical machine design course, this project involved the comprehensive design of a 1250kVA transformer using detailed calculation methods. Key parameters calculated included voltage per turn, magnetic loading, core diameter, window area, LV & HV winding turns, layout design for windings, percentage reactance and resistance, weight estimations for core and copper, loss calculations, and performance metrics like regulation and efficiency. The design also encompassed tank specifications and temperature rise analysis.

Tools: Detailed Calculation Methods, Electrical Machine Design Principles.

Outcome: Complete design specification and performance analysis of a 1250kVA transformer.

Construction of Digital Thermometer Using a Thermistor and Microcontroller

This experiment showcased the construction and functionality of a digital thermometer. It utilized a thermistor, whose resistance varies significantly with temperature. The PIC16F877A microcontroller was programmed to read the thermistor's resistance, convert this value into a temperature reading, and display it on an LM016L LCD screen, demonstrating a straightforward yet effective digital thermometer.

Tools: PIC16F877A Microcontroller, LM016L LCD Display, Resistor, Capacitor, Button, Voltmeter, DC Voltage (5V), Thermistor.

Outcome: A functional simulated digital thermometer capable of reading and displaying temperature.
